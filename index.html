Cyber Awareness

Essential steps to protect yourself online. Share with your friends and classmates.

Enable 2-Factor Authentication

Turn on 2FA for email, banking, and social media. Prefer authenticator apps over SMS.

Spot Phishing

Check sender domain, avoid urgent money/OTP requests, hover links before clicking.

Secure Devices

Use strong passcodes, auto-updates, and device encryption. Never install cracked apps.

Privacy on Social Media

Limit public info. Disable contact syncing and location history where not needed.

Financial Safety

Never share UPI PIN/OTP. Verify handles. Use in-app support for disputes.

Report & Preserve Evidence

Take screenshots, note timestamps, keep emails/SMS. Report promptly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this portal a government website?

No. This is an Institute-only portal for awareness and preliminary reporting. For national-level complaints, use the official government portal.

Can I report anonymously?

Yes. However, investigation and response may be limited without contact details.

What happens after I submit a report?

Your report is assigned a Case ID and shared with the campus Cyber Cell. They may contact you for more details.
